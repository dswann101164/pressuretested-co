The Signal Score

Four signals.
One number.

The Signal Score measures the four things YouTube's algorithm actually uses to decide whether to distribute your channel. Not opinions. Not best practices. The data.

CTR Signal 0–30 pts

Click-through rate measures whether your thumbnail and title earn the click when YouTube shows your video. Below 4% means packaging is failing. This is the most common channel killer.

What is YouTube CTR? →
Impression Depth 0–25 pts

How deeply YouTube is distributing your channel. Low impression volume means the algorithm hasn't committed to serving your content — usually because CTR hasn't earned that trust yet.

Views Per Impression 0–25 pts

How efficiently your channel converts distribution into actual views. VPI combines CTR and watch time — a low VPI means you're wasting the impressions YouTube is giving you.

What is VPI? →
Upload Velocity 0–20 pts

Publishing consistency. The algorithm learns your channel faster with a regular data stream. Gaps reset momentum. Two videos per week is the threshold — anything below that slows classification.
