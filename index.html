Question 01 — You
Who are you professionally?
Your field and how long you've been in it. e.g. IT architect, 34 years — or CPA, 20 years in public sector
Question 02 — Passion
What do you want the channel to be about?
Could be your day job — or something entirely different. Both are valid. Pick a category or describe it in your own words. No YouTube jargon required.

Question 03 — Authority
What's the specific problem you solve that most people in your field can't?
Not what you do — what you specifically do that others struggle with. This is your unfair advantage on camera.
Question 04 — Audience
Who suffers most when that problem doesn't get solved?
Don't think demographics. Think pain. Who loses sleep over the problem you just described? That person is your viewer.
Question 05 — The Teaching Hour
If a younger version of you asked for one hour — what would you teach them?
This surfaces your content naturally. What do you wish someone had told you 10 years ago?
Question 06 — Where You Are
Do you have a YouTube channel already?
Honest answer only. The diagnostic needs to know what it's working with.
What happens next: We derive your niche from your answers — you never have to name it. You'll see how crowded your lane is, then land in the Signal Diagnostic with your position already loaded.
