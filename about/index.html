Why I built this

I've spent 34 years in enterprise IT — Azure architecture, Power BI, KQL, DAX, systems design. I know how to build things that work. I also know what AI displacement looks like from the inside of a corporate environment, because I'm watching it happen in real time from my desk at Pinnacle Financial Partners.

The window is 12–18 months for a lot of professionals in my position. Managers, IT workers, accountants, lawyers — the jobs that mid-level corporate America runs on. Not the jobs at the bottom, not the jobs at the top. The ones in the middle, where AI is most efficient at replacing human judgment.

I decided to build something before the decision gets made for me. Not after.

Why YouTube

YouTube is the only platform where 34 years of specific expertise — the kind you can't fake, the kind that comes from watching a dozen technology cycles come and go — is actually a competitive advantage. The 25-year-olds building AI content channels don't have what I have. They haven't been through a corporate environment at scale. They haven't managed budgets with real consequences. They haven't had to explain technical decisions to a CFO who doesn't care about the technology.

The professional over 40 who is watching AI displacement coming — that person is being served content by people who've never been in their shoes. That's the gap. That's the channel.

The core principle

Every YouTube coach in this space sells a guarantee. If the channel grows — they take credit. If it doesn't — you didn't do the work. The customer absorbs all the risk. The coach absorbs none.

I built the diagnostic tool to remove that escape hatch. Baseline before engagement. Measurable delta after. Either the numbers moved or they didn't. That's not a weaker promise — for a professional over 40 who's been sold snake oil before, that's a stronger one.
