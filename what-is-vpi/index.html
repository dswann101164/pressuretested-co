YouTube Glossary

What is
VPI?

Views Per Impression is the metric most YouTube creators have never heard of. It may be the real reason your channel isn't growing — and it measures something CTR alone can't see.

Definition
VPI (Views Per Impression) measures how efficiently a YouTube channel converts the distribution it receives into actual views. It combines your CTR and watch time signals into a single efficiency ratio.

The formula

VPI = Views ÷ Impressions
Example: 100 views from 2,000 impressions = VPI of 0.05 (5%)

VPI is essentially your CTR expressed as a decimal. But its value isn't in the number itself — it's in what changes when you compare it across videos on the same channel, and what it reveals when it diverges unexpectedly from your CTR.

How VPI differs from CTR

CTR measures whether someone clicked your thumbnail. VPI measures whether the combination of your thumbnail, title, and content quality is producing sustainable views from the impressions YouTube serves.

A video can have a strong CTR and weak VPI. This happens when a thumbnail earns clicks out of curiosity or confusion — but viewers click away immediately when the content doesn't match the promise of the packaging. YouTube reads the short watch time and reduces distribution.

VPI captures the full conversion — from impression to click to watched view — in one number. CTR only captures half of it.

What causes an anomalous VPI?

Sometimes a video shows a VPI that's much higher than its CTR would predict. This usually means the video is receiving external traffic — direct links, embeds, shares outside of YouTube — rather than browse feed distribution.

What's a good VPI on YouTube?

For channels under 50,000 subscribers, a VPI above 0.05 (5%) indicates healthy conversion. Below 0.02 (2%) suggests either very low CTR, very poor watch time retention, or both.

The more useful comparison is relative VPI across your own videos. A video with consistently higher VPI than your channel average is performing well and should be studied for what made it work. A video with significantly lower VPI is a drag on channel authority.

VPI and channel authority

YouTube's algorithm uses aggregate VPI across your channel as a signal of quality. A channel that consistently converts impressions into views — and keeps those viewers watching — earns more distribution. A channel with erratic or low VPI gets tested less frequently because the algorithm has learned it doesn't reliably convert.

Low VPI Channel

Gets impressions → few clicks → short watches → algorithm reduces distribution → fewer impressions → fewer views. The cycle compounds downward.

High VPI Channel

Gets impressions → strong clicks → full watches → algorithm increases distribution → more impressions → more views. The cycle compounds upward.

How to improve your VPI

If your CTR is low: Fix the thumbnail and title first. VPI can't improve if people aren't clicking. The packaging problem is upstream of everything else.

If your CTR is acceptable but VPI is still low: The problem is watch time. Your video isn't delivering on the promise of the thumbnail and title — viewers are clicking and leaving. The hook (first 30–45 seconds) needs to immediately validate the click and establish why this video is worth their time.

If VPI is anomalously high on one video: Investigate the traffic source. High VPI from external traffic isn't a bad thing — but it means your YouTube browse performance may be weaker than the aggregate number suggests.
